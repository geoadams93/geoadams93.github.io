Alsamin

This document serves as a primer for the fantasy world Alsamin, where this D&D campaign takes place. Within it will describe a brief description and history of the world as well as the major nations and people. Knowledge within can help you decide what your characters background, motivations, and relationships are.

The world

 

Alsamin is the name of the world and the sole continent that makes up the livable land. Sailors who attempt to sail beyond are eventually met with The Roil areas of the world where the laws of nature and reality start to bend and travel beyond is not possible.

The current age is known as The Silver Age named such as a marked decline from The Golden Age, when Gods walked among mortals wielding the power of Divinity, which allowed them to shape reality at will. The Gods disappeared ten thousand years ago, although many traces of their existence remain in the world, including the power of the clerics that worship them.

The Four Empires

The focus of the world is on the Four Empires whose politics and conflicts shape the lives of everyone. They dominate the one and only continent of the world, although small kingdoms lay within their spheres of influence yet remain nominally independent.

Catatan

Catatan is the youngest of the four, and literally means union in Elvish. It is a collection of crowns held together in personal union by a king. Carrying the ancient traditions of the Elves alongside the customs of the Men of the North it is a cosmopolitan entity that is only considered to be an Empire in the loosest sense. Existing for decades in an interregnum, scheming members of the royal council direct the affairs of the nation.

The people of Catatan are made up of many ancestries. Gnomes, Elves, Halflings, and many others call Catatan home. The vast majority of the populace are Humans and Half-Elves. The majority language is Elvish, descended from ancient Celestial and Sylvan.

The Karani Empire

The Karani Empire is old and proud. Conquerors who claim descent from the original human civilizations, they preside over the largest of the Four Empires. Most of their land is arid and uninhabited. The majority of the population clings to the banks of the great World River.

The Empire is ruled by a Tamar, the title for the absolute monarch descended from Tamar the Great. The Karani people are proud of the technological ingenuity and cultural influence they wield. They were the ones to first invent firearms, their language Common - is the lingua franca of the known world, and they claim the most relics and temples of the past Golden Age.

The people are mostly Humans, however the vast size of the empire means many smaller groups of all races live within.

The Raabatine Republic

The smallest of the empires, both in terms of land and population, the Raabatine Republics power is in its great wealth. Founded by priests fleeing the Karani Empire due to their views that their worship was being twisted and corrupted for political reasons, they set out to the dangerous Raaba archipelago.

They have an extreme laizzez-faire attitude toward governance. As long as they are able to carry out their holy duties, they are ambivalent towards the laws of the republic. This has led to abundant wealth flowing through the republics, but slavery and plutocratic corruption is rife.

The republic is so named as the rulership is nominally done so via collective voting from the heads of the major temples, however merchant lords hold all the real sway. Anyone of sufficient means may claim to be a lord, limited only by the ability to protect that claim. The Silk Lord, Sugar Lord and many others claim these titles and monopolies. All however envy the power of the mysterious Spice King, who has never been physically seen.

All different people of all backgrounds live in the republics, but many reflect the Karani background of the original settlers.

The Rao Dynasty

The Dwarven Rao Dynasty, Rao being Dragon in Dwarvish claims divine descent from their chief god Father Mountain. The theocratic state is said to be ruled by the cyclically reincarnating souls of Father Mountains dragon sons incarnated into their Emperor.

Generally isolationist, the vast majority of the population are Dwarves. Dragonborn exist within the empire, as do minorities of other groups. Dwarvish is the common language, although many of the populace understand the liturgical language Draconic.

Faiths

There are many religious traditions in the world, and even so there are those who profess no faith. Within the Rao Dynasty or Karani Empire there are state religions, but even so faith is a personal thing to the denizens of the world.

The Old Gods

This is the worship of the various entities that claimed Divinity in the Golden Age. This is the state religion of the Karani Empire, and the faith expressed by the settlers in the Raabatine Republics. Some example deities are:

Magapala: Goddess of water and the sea. Depicted as a jovial merfolk queen. She is commonly worshipped by sailors and merchants.

Karor: God of stone and strength. Depicted as a stone giant with a grim countenance. Commonly worshipped by builders and soldiers.

Muraga: Goddess of the dead and dying. Depicted as a three headed dog feasting on the dead. Commonly worshipped by those who seek an end to their suffering or the desperate.

There are uncountable Old Gods, some representing local areas to those whose portfolio are common and widespread. Usually, laypersons give homage to many Old Gods, while a cleric may serve one their whole life.

The Path of Ascension

A religious revival among those with Elvish ancestry in the Catatan, this movement teaches its followers to strive to live a perfect life. Those who manage to do so through acts of goodness and charity may gather the strength to ascend to the Angelos, becoming a celestial and leaving the physical world. Those who follow this faith worship no deities, but many clerics and paladins give homage to the core concepts of this faith and manage to draw holy power.

Father Mountain

The religion of the Dwarves, they worship Father Mountain who they claim created them and their mountainous home. They also give reverence to his two sons who took the form of two great metallic dragons. When the two brothers fought over how to best carry out their fathers wishes, he forced the two to be mortal and rule the dwarven people as a humble dwarf. The most centralized and organized religion in the world, it is generally only followed by dwarves. There are, however, syncretic movements that seek to consolidate the worship of Father Mountain with the Old Gods.

Your Story

You are an Adventurer. This is a regulated profession in the world governed by the Adventurers Guild. The guild is headquartered in the City of Tamars Tomb, located within the Kingsbane Swamp located in the neutral territories between the Four Empires.

A war has broken out between the Four Empires, with The Lao and Karani Empires allying against the Catatan and Raabatine Republics. Per the laws of the guild, you and your party have been summoned to attend a summit in Tamars Tomb or be branded outlaws.
